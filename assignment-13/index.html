Josie Arganbright Art

I have always been drawn to the human experience, to the small moments that aren’t normally seen. I want to capture the mundane and intimate moments experienced in everyday life and shine a spotlight on them to visualize the feelings our shared human experience. My work dramatizes snapshots of private moments that are not usually considered significant or important.
Paint is the main medium that I prefer. Painting allows me to layer color to build depth, control expressive lighting, and add metaphorical elements—all of which help evoke emotions I wish to convey. The rich history behind paint is inspiring and looking at other painters throughout history has had a huge impact on my work; the idea of working with light was strongly inspired by Edward Hopper and I look at Francis Bacon's work whenever I want to experiment with brushwork. I have spent hours developing my own style and experimenting with how different variations of brush strokes and colors can be used in a piece and what kind of visual and emotional effect they have.
I have made it a long way in my art journey, but I have a long way to go. I have just started to work on progressively larger surfaces, which is what I feel is best for my style and the impact of the piece, but which also pose specific challenges that I am learning how to solve. In the past year, I have started to really develop a strong sense of style. While it is getting better with everything I paint, I can see elements I want to expand, strengthen, or leave behind.
